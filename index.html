Who we are

A youth-led mission built on one belief:

one conversation changes everything.

We are a team of young people who've seen firsthand what happens when teens feel too scared to speak up — and too ashamed to ask for help. Bridging the Silence is our answer: a 6-week programme equipping parents across ASEAN with the practical tools to recognise distress and start the conversations that matter most.

  • Equipping parents with practical communication tools
  • Connecting families to expert mental health guidance
  • Reaching teens through the adults closest to them
  • Building an ASEAN-wide community of support

Why this matters

Youth suicide in ASEAN is rising.
The warning signs are being missed — at home.

While overall suicide rates declined in the WHO South-East Asia region, youth rates continue to climb. The critical intervention window is ages 13–18. Parents are the first responders — but most haven't been equipped to act.

73%

of youth mental health deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries — where resources are most scarce

13–18

The highest-risk window for mental health decline. Academic pressure, identity formation, and emotional instability converge — daily, at home

6–16%

of young adults report suicidal ideation — most without any professional support or family intervention
